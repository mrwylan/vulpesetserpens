# Vision

**Every sound creator can turn any recording into a perfect loop — instantly, in the browser.**

We envision a world where the creative moment is never broken by tedious, technical busywork. Finding the right loop point in a sample today means manual waveform scrubbing, trial-and-error edits, and repeated listening — a frustrating interruption to the creative flow.

Vulpesetserpens removes that friction entirely. The tool listens, analyzes, and surfaces loop candidates automatically, so the creator can stay in flow and focus on what matters: making music.

## Who this is for

Sound creators work at very different scales — and all of them loop.

**The sound designer** works at the micro level. They are building an instrument patch and need a seamless sustain loop buried inside a single recorded note: a few milliseconds of waveform that must stitch back to itself without a click, repeating indefinitely as long as a key is held. The loop boundary is everything; the musical content is almost incidental.

**The musician** works at the note or chord level. They have a recording of an instrument playing a phrase and want to isolate a single clean event — one note, one chord — that can stand on its own and loop naturally. The challenge is finding the musical boundaries that feel right, not just the technically smooth ones.

**The producer** works at the structural level. They have beats, bars, and phrases and want to carve them into building blocks that can be freely rearranged, layered, and triggered. A loop here is a compositional unit: it needs to be rhythmically tight, temporally coherent, and ready to drop into a session.

## The shared problem

All three profiles face the same underlying obstacle: the loop boundary. A loop that clicks is useless. Finding a click-free boundary by hand is slow, error-prone, and pulls the creator out of the creative state where their best decisions get made.

Vulpesetserpens automates the search so that every type of creator — whatever their scale of work — gets back to creating faster.

The application is designed for creative people. It should feel inspiring to use — not like a utility, but like an instrument.
